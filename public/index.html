GENERATION TEACH

Catalyzing the next generation of great teachers across the US

The Issue: The Academic Opportunity Gap

In our country (the US), if you’re a child living in poverty or a child of color the odds are your schooling is inadequate. In fact, for many students the promise of education as a pathway to opportunity is less of a reality today than it was two decades ago.
You may not know that:


Fact #1

Every year, 1.3 million students drop out of high school in the United States. More than half are students of color, and most are children of low-income families.

Fact #2

More than 80% of black and 75% of Hispanic 4th and 8th grade students could not read or compete at grade level in 2013.

Fact #3

Last year, one in three Black students and four in ten Hispanic and Native American students did not graduate from high school in four years.

This has to change. The students whose life’s paths are dependent on a good education deserve better.

The Solution: Great Teachers

Research has finally caught up with common sense. For decades, researchers (and anyone who went to school) knew the difference between a strong and weak teacher, but they couldn’t actually pinpoint why or how this mattered. Now, research has confirmed what we’ve all known: that the quality of a teacher is the most important factor in raising student achievement. We also know that:


Fact #1

Teachers matter more to student learning than any other in-school factor.

Fact #2

Students with effective teachers achieve 3x the academic gains than those with less effective teachers, regardless of a student’s background.

Fact #3

If underserved students have a good teacher 4 years in a row we can eliminate the achievement gap.

Helping teachers get better earlier is among the most pressing needs in American schooling.
And it has to happen now.

The Vision


Our vision is one in which every child in every school is taught by a great teacher. To achieve our vision, we plan to radically change when and how young people, (like yourself), are attracted to, prepared for, and placed into the teaching profession.




No reform in education is more critical. To create great schools, to provide equal opportunities, to unlock the potential of education for all children, we need great teachers. But not just one or two teachers or even twenty or thirty.






Addressing the challenges in public schooling are too significant a task to tackle alone. We need a, new generation of diverse, talented, and committed young people together choosing to teach.




We need a generation of new teachers who share common experiences and practices; who are prepared for the rewards and rigor of teaching; and who collectively, through a shared commitment to each other and their profession, change the lives of all students, everywhere.

TEACH THIS SUMMER


This summer, Generation Teach is launching its inaugural Teaching Fellowship in partnership with Denver Public Schools (DPS).
The GT Summer Fellowship is a five-week program for high school and undergraduate students who are interested in exploring the world of teaching while still students themselves.

You will learn


As a GT Resident, you will receive over 100 hours of training before the summer begins. Everything you need to know before you step into the classroom will be covered, including how to:

  • Deliver Common Core curricula
  • Provide specific directions for student behavior and academics
  • Develop effective routines and procedures
  • Implement daily lesson activities that are well sequenced and move students toward mastery
  • Accurately check for whether students understand key content

You will teach


For 6 weeks, from 8 a.m. – 3 p.m. You will have your own classroom. You will teach a class of 5-10 low-income, rising seventh graders in a subject of your choice: literature, math, science, or social studies. You will not be a teaching assistant. You will not be a teacher’s aide. You will be a teacher.

You will be mentored


A Teacher Coach – a professional “master” public school teacher will be your dedicated mentor for the summer. As part of a unique learning community, you and two other GT Residents will receive feedback, support, and guidance for the entire summer. The support is intensive; the practice comprehensive; the expectations stratospheric, but we believe you can become an exemplary educator by being one now -- while you are still a student yourself.

You will make an impact


By the end of the summer you will be able to track the individual and collective learning of your students and reflect on your impact and opportunities for growth as a teacher. You will evaluate and be evaluated. You will provide and receive feedback. And you’ll see for yourself how to effect positive, long-lasting change.

You will be part of a new Generation


As a GT Resident you are taking the first, critical step towards joining and building a new generation of young teachers. With a cohort of highly talented and dedicated individuals, you will, collectively, play a pivotal role in improving the odds of success for your students and the communities in which you work. You will also change society’s perception of how teachers are (and should) be prepared and supported in the US.

FAQs

Who can apply?
Any undergraduate student (or recently graduated high school student) from anywhere in the country can apply to join this summer’s Denver cohort. The GT Summer Fellowship is highly selective. We are looking for individuals who are creative, passionate, hard working, and who want to continuously improve their ability to be the best at what they do. You do not need to have a background in education or instructional experience; we’ll teach you that. You do need to have a record of achievement and a history of perseverance.


Where is the GT Residency?
Our Denver Fellowship is based in Denver Public Schools at the Lake campus, home of Lake International School and STRIVE Prep-Lake. Fellowships are hosted by public and charter schools invested in developing their future teachers and leaders. By working in and with a local school, you will become part of the same school and community in which your students live and learn.


When is the GT Residency?
GT Resident Training: June 9-20, 2014, 8am – 6pm
Six-Week Session: June 23–August 2, 2014, 7:30am – 6pm
Wrap-Up and Evaluation: August 4-6, 2014, 8am – 6pm


What is the salary?
As a GT Resident you will receive a stipend of $2,500
for your nine-week participation.


How are fellows selected?
All candidates must complete and submit an online application. As part of your application, you will submit a resume and transcripts and answer three essay questions. If you are advanced in the selection process, you will be asked to submit a sample teaching video. Applicants will either move forward to a phone or in-person interview, or will be notified that their application has been declined. Interviews will be conducted with a GT Program Director. Candidates are typically notified of their status within two weeks of their final interview.

Reinventing Teacher Preparation

Generation Teach was launched in 2014 with a singular vision—to reinvent teacher preparation in the U.S. Through a multi-year high school and college program that extends through the first two years of teaching, Generation Teach is building the career pathway that prepares and supports students before, during, and after they enter the teaching profession.

Through a unique partnership with Relay Graduate School of Education, Generation Teach is providing a ground-breaking path for young people to pursue teaching, master critical skills for success in urban classrooms, develop a record of achievement, and leverage their collective impact in schools, districts, and cities across the country.


In 2015, we will be expanding to serve many more cities and introducing new initiatives, more programs, and credit relief with leading graduate schools of education. Stay connected to learn about these offerings.
